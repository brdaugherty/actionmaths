# Perspective Journey: Three Paradigms of Reality

This interactive experience explores how our understanding of space, time, and probability changes as we shift our perspective. It demonstrates three distinct mathematical models used to describe the same quantum event.

## How Probability is Involved

Probability is visualized differently in each of the three paradigms. Use the slider to morph between them.

### 1. The Classical Paradigm (Space + Time)
- **Visual:** A localized cyan ripple expands across a flat plane.
- **Role of Probability:** Probability is a "moving event." It travels through empty space at a fixed speed.
- **The "Now":** Only the current front of the wave exists. The past is gone, and the future is not yet written.

### 2. The Block Universe (Spacetime)
- **Visual:** The plane morphs into a 3D volume. The Z-axis becomes **Time**.
- **Role of Probability:** The wave is no longer moving; it is a **static 3D object** called a **Light Cone**. 
- **The Perspective Shift:** In this view, past, present, and future exist simultaneously. Probability isn't something that "happens"—it is a geometric feature of a 4D block of spacetime.

### 3. The Relational Paradigm (Entanglement)
- **Visual:** The rigid grid shatters into a chaotic, floating network of nodes.
- **Role of Probability:** Space is revealed as a **Relational Network**. The "distance" the wave traveled is an illusion. 
- **The Perspective Shift:** The "collapse" of a wave is visualized as a physical, geometric bridge (an **Entanglement Thread**) forming directly between two nodes. In this paradigm, "instantaneous" action at a distance is possible because the nodes are directly connected in the underlying network topology, regardless of how "far" they appear to be.

## Theoretical Significance: Emergent Spacetime
This journey illustrates the concept of **Emergence**. It shows that what we perceive as "Time" or "Distance" depends entirely on the dimensionality of our perspective.
- At low dimensions, we see particles moving through space.
- At higher dimensions, we see static geometric histories.
- At the relational level, we see that space itself is built out of quantum connections (entanglement).
